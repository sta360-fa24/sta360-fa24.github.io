Practice: exponential families and variable transformation

Important

This lab will not be graded.

Exercise 1

Let \(y | \theta \sim binary(\theta)\).

  1. Write down the joint density of several observations, \(p(y_1, \ldots y_n | \theta)\).
  2. Identify the sufficient statistic \(t(y)\), and the transform \(\phi = f(\theta)\) such that the joint density \(p(y_1,\ldots y_n | \phi)\) can be written as \(h(y) c(\phi) e^{\phi t(y)}\).
  3. Write down the prior \(p(\phi|n_0, t_0)\).
  4. Confirm that \(f(\theta)\) is monotonic and invertible. Next, using the one-line formula, \(p_\theta(\theta) = p_{\phi}(f(\theta))|\frac{dy}{dx}|\), show that the prior, \(p(\theta | n_0, t_0)\) is a beta density.
